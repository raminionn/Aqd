LANDLORD AND TENANT LAW IN PAKISTAN — KEY PROVISIONS

APPLICABLE LAW
Tenancy in Pakistan is primarily governed by provincial rent restriction 
ordinances. The main ones are the Sindh Rented Premises Ordinance 1979, 
the Punjab Rented Premises Act 2009, and the Khyber Pakhtunkhwa Urban 
Rent Restriction Ordinance 1959. These laws protect tenants from arbitrary 
eviction and rent increases.

SECURITY DEPOSIT
The standard security deposit in Pakistan is 2 months of rent. Deposits 
exceeding 3 months of rent are above standard and should be flagged. 
The contract must clearly state the conditions under which the deposit 
can be withheld. If the contract allows the landlord to withhold the 
deposit for vague reasons like "damages" without a process for dispute, 
this is a red flag. The deposit should be refundable within 30 days of 
vacating if the premises are in good condition.

RENT INCREASE
Under Pakistani rent laws, landlords cannot increase rent arbitrarily 
during a tenancy term. Rent increases are typically regulated and must 
follow a process. Contracts that allow the landlord to increase rent 
at any time without notice are against standard practice. Standard 
contracts specify that rent can only be revised at renewal, with at 
least 1 month notice.

NOTICE PERIOD FOR TERMINATION
The standard notice period for ending a tenancy in Pakistan is 1 month 
for monthly tenancies. Fixed-term contracts should specify what happens 
at the end of the term. Contracts that allow the landlord to evict with 
less than 1 month notice are non-standard and should be flagged.

MAINTENANCE AND REPAIRS
The standard position in Pakistan is that major structural repairs are 
the landlord's responsibility. Minor day-to-day maintenance is the 
tenant's responsibility. Contracts that place all maintenance including 
structural repairs on the tenant are one-sided and should be flagged.

SUBLETTING
Most rental contracts in Pakistan prohibit subletting without the 
landlord's written consent. This is standard and not a red flag.

UTILITY CONNECTIONS
Contracts should specify whether utilities (electricity, gas, water) 
are included in the rent or paid separately by the tenant. Vague 
contracts that do not address utilities create room for disputes.

EVICTION PROCESS
Under Pakistani law, a landlord cannot forcibly evict a tenant without 
a court order. Any clause that allows the landlord to enter the premises 
and remove the tenant without a court process is unenforceable but 
still worth flagging as a red flag because it signals an aggressive landlord.

WHAT IS COMMONLY MISSING FROM PAKISTANI RENTAL AGREEMENTS
Dispute resolution mechanism specifying which court or arbitration applies.
Inventory list of existing fixtures and their condition at the start of tenancy.
Clear process for returning the security deposit.
Specification of who pays for which utilities.
What happens if the property is sold during the tenancy.

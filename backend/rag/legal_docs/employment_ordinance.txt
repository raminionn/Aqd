PAKISTANI EMPLOYMENT LAW — KEY PROVISIONS FOR CONTRACT ANALYSIS

Source: Industrial and Commercial Employment (Standing Orders) Ordinance 1968, 
and general employment practice in Pakistan.

PROBATION PERIOD
The standard probation period in Pakistan is 3 months. The maximum legally 
acceptable probation period is 6 months. Any probation period exceeding 6 months 
is non-standard and should be flagged. During probation, either party may terminate 
without notice in most arrangements, though this should be explicitly stated.

NOTICE PERIOD
The standard notice period for confirmed employees in Pakistan is 1 month. 
Senior roles may have 2 to 3 month notice periods. Notice periods exceeding 
3 months are unusual and may be used to trap employees. Notice periods should 
be mutual — the same for both employer and employee. If the contract specifies 
a longer notice for the employee than the employer, this is a red flag.

TERMINATION WITHOUT CAUSE
Under Pakistani law, an employer can terminate a confirmed employee but must 
provide the contractual notice period or pay in lieu. Contracts that allow 
the employer to terminate immediately without cause and without pay in lieu 
are not standard and should be flagged.

GRATUITY
Employees who complete a full year of service are entitled to gratuity under 
Pakistani law. The standard rate is 30 days of last drawn basic salary per year 
of service. Contracts that exclude or limit gratuity entitlement are reducing 
a legal right and should be flagged as a red flag.

OVERTIME
Under the Factories Act 1934, workers are entitled to double the ordinary rate 
of pay for overtime work. White-collar contracts often exclude overtime. 
If the contract says overtime is not compensated or is included in salary, 
this should be flagged as something to clarify.

ANNUAL LEAVE
The standard annual leave entitlement in Pakistan is 14 days per year for 
most categories of workers. Contracts offering fewer than 14 days of annual 
leave are below the standard. Casual leave of 10 days and sick leave of 8 days 
per year are also standard.

BASIC SALARY VS GROSS SALARY
In Pakistani employment contracts, salary is often split between basic salary 
and allowances (house rent allowance, medical allowance, conveyance allowance). 
Gratuity, provident fund, and EOBI contributions are calculated on basic salary, 
not gross. Contracts that do not specify what portion is basic are vague and 
should be clarified.

EOBI
Employees Oldage Benefits Institution (EOBI) contributions are mandatory in 
Pakistan for companies with 5 or more employees. The employer contributes 5% 
of minimum wage and the employee contributes 1%. Contracts that do not mention 
EOBI are not necessarily deficient but the employee should confirm this is 
being handled.

PROVIDENT FUND
Many Pakistani employers offer provident fund as a benefit where both employer 
and employee contribute an equal percentage (typically 8.33% of basic salary). 
This is not legally mandatory for all employers but is standard in medium to 
large companies. Absence of provident fund in a large company contract is 
worth flagging.

WORKPLACE DISCRIMINATION AND HARASSMENT
The Protection Against Harassment of Women at Workplace Act 2010 applies to 
all workplaces in Pakistan. Employers are required to have an inquiry committee. 
Contracts that explicitly mention this policy are better practice. Absence is 
not a red flag but worth noting.

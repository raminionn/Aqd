NDA STANDARDS IN PAKISTAN — WHAT IS NORMAL AND WHAT IS A RED FLAG

WHAT AN NDA IS
A Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA) is a contract where one or both parties 
agree not to share confidential information with third parties. In Pakistan, 
NDAs are common in employment, freelance, partnership, and investor contexts. 
They are enforceable under the Contract Act 1872.

MUTUAL VS ONE-SIDED NDA
A mutual NDA protects both parties' confidential information equally. 
A one-sided NDA only protects one party — typically the company. 
One-sided NDAs are standard when a company is sharing information with 
a contractor or employee. They are a red flag only if the definition of 
confidential information is excessively broad.

DEFINITION OF CONFIDENTIAL INFORMATION
The NDA must clearly define what counts as confidential. A definition 
that says "all information of any kind shared between the parties" is 
dangerously broad. A good definition limits confidential information 
to specific categories — business plans, client lists, financial data, 
proprietary technology, trade secrets. If the definition is so broad 
that it could include public information or general knowledge, it is 
a red flag.

DURATION
Standard NDA duration in Pakistan is 2 to 3 years. NDAs lasting more 
than 5 years are aggressive. Perpetual NDAs — those with no expiry date 
— are common in employment contexts for genuine trade secrets but should 
be scrutinised. A perpetual NDA for general business information is 
aggressive and worth flagging.

EXCLUSIONS FROM CONFIDENTIALITY
A well-drafted NDA always includes exclusions — information that is 
already public, information the recipient already knew, information 
received from a third party without restriction, and information 
required to be disclosed by law. If the NDA has no exclusions, it 
is poorly drafted and a red flag.

RETURN OF INFORMATION
Good NDAs require the receiving party to return or destroy all 
confidential information when the relationship ends. If the NDA 
does not address what happens to confidential information after 
termination, this is a missing clause.

REMEDIES FOR BREACH
NDAs in Pakistan typically state that breach will cause irreparable 
harm and entitle the disclosing party to seek an injunction without 
needing to prove financial damage. This is standard. NDAs that 
specify very large fixed penalties for breach should be flagged 
as the amount should be proportionate.

WHAT IS COMMONLY MISSING FROM PAKISTANI NDAs
No exclusions for publicly available information.
No process for handling legally compelled disclosure.
No return or destruction of information clause.
No specification of which court has jurisdiction.
No clear definition of what counts as a breach.
